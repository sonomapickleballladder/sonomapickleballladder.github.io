When:

  • This Fall Season starts on September 15th 2025, and runs through November 30th 2025.

Where:

  • Players decide where to play their matches, but typically central Sonoma County locations like Santa Rosa or Rohnert Park.

Frequency:

  • Each round (set of matches) runs for 1.5 weeks, and will start on Mondays or Thursdays in alternating fashion.

Confirming Availability:

  • In order to be included in the groups each round, you will need to confirm that you are available before the round starts. 'Being available' entails being able to offer multiple possible time-windows in which you can play, and being willing to play after standard work hours (many participants are not yet retired 😊).

  • If you don't have much availability for a particular round or don't wish to play because of injury or anything else, you don't have to do anything (you are assumed unavailable if you don't confirm).

Groupings:

  • Players will be grouped in groups of 4 at the start of each round. These are determined by a combination of Standings position and availability. Group 1 will be comprised of the 4 highest-standing available players. Group 2 will be the next 4, and so on. (Note: if the number of available players is not a multiple of 4, the last group will have to be completed with subs).

  • Groupings can be found on the Groups Page. They will be updated on the first day of each round and you will be notified (by email) as soon as they are ready.

Scheduling:

  • Once you receive the email at the start of each round which tells you the new groups are ready, identify your group on the Groups Page and initiate communication with everyone via a single email or text thread to coordinate when and where you will play.

  • If you are unable to land on a viable time for all 4 of you, your group can get a sub (or multiple subs if necessary) to replace whoever is least available. A sub may also be called in if a player has not responded to the others' efforts to schedule after 2 days notice or more.

Unforeseen Circumstances:

    If one or more players in your group get sick, injured, or are otherwise unable to play due to exceptional circumstances, the following applies:

  • The missing players drop 1 place in the Ladder Standings for the following round (as they would if they had otherwise not been available).

  • The rest of you should try to a get a sub (and reschedule if necessary). If this is not possible – perhaps it is the last day of the round (pro tip: don't schedule your match for the last day of the round) – your group will not play and everyone in it will drop 1 spot in the Standings).

Sub(stitute)s:

    As I'm sure you gathered from reading the above, there will inevitably be groups that end up needing subs throughout the course of the Ladder Season.

  • Subs do not have to be in the Ladder, but should ideally be of a similar skill level.

  • The Subs Page can help facilitate the process of finding a sub. Anyone interested in subbing for a particular round can submit their name, and you can see everyone who is offering.

  • When submitting scores, please name the sub as 'FirstName LastName (Sub)' (replacing the name of the person they subbed in for in the appropriate cell of the spreadsheet if submitting via spreadsheet). Example: John Doe (Sub).

  • Subs' scores do not count for those they are subbing in for. (Remaining players' scores count as normal).

  • In exceptional circumstances, I as the Ladder Administrator may allow a sub's scores to count for that sub if they were unable to play in their own group, but were able to fill in for a missing player in an adjacent group.

Punctuality:

  • Please don’t be late! Be conscious that others in your group have arranged their schedule to play at that specific time. If you’re late, you may be significantly inconveniencing or frustrating them.

  • To be fair to all the other players, unless it’s an emergency, or there are extenuating circumstances, the following penalties apply for arriving late past the scheduled start time:

    15-30 mins late :: your opponents get a 3 point head start in every game to 11 (a 5 point head start in every game to 15).

    30-45 mins late :: your opponents get an 5 point head start in every game to 11 (a 8 point head start in every game to 15).

    45+ mins late :: your opponents get an 7 point head start in every game to 11 (a 11 point head start in every game to 15).

    No-show :: you will not be able to play the next round. (Please report to me if anyone in your group is a no-show so I can enforce this 😊).

Format:

  • Round-robin, 6 games to 11, win by 2, (don't switch sides mid-game).

  • If all 4 players in your group agree to it, you may play 3 games to 15 (switch sides at 8), or 3 games to 11 (switch at 6).

  • The #1 seed highest standing player (order of Standings on the Groups Page is highest to lowest from left to right) shall play with the #4 seed first game, then with the #3 seed, and lastly with the #2 seed.

  • In the default 6 games to 11 format, repeat this sequence twice, and play on the opposite side of the court the second time you play with the same person.

  • Note: As per public court policy, you will need to rotate off between games if there are players waiting, and then you can rotate on to the next available court.

Submitting Your Scores:

  • Please keep track of your scores after each game finishes and make sure everyone in your group agrees with them at the end of play.

  • It is every group's responsibility to submit their scores before the end of each round, as they are used to compute the new Standings for the next round. Failure to submit on time will result in all players in the group dropping one ranking spot (the same as if they hadn't played). Since nobody wants this to happen, it is a good practice to submit them the same day right after playing. You may want to nominate one player from your group to do the submission.

  • To submit scores, please visit the Submit Scores Page. You will have the choice to submit via form submission or enter them directly into the Scores Spreadsheet. Submitting by spreadsheet is preferable if you have access to a computer (it is not very usable on a phone).

  • Please look at the Scores Spreadsheet the day before the start of the next round if possible to check that your group's scores appear correct. Once the next round starts, they will not be able to be changed.

Moving Up or Down:

  • New Standings will be formed each round based on the previous round's results.

  • There are two operations which are performed to calculate these):

  1. Firstly, the players who played that round are re-ranked based on their seed number (relative rank within group) and how they finished in their group (see 'Players Playing Re-ranked' rows in the Results sheet). You finish 1st(/4th) in your group by having the highest(/lowest) point differential (points won – points lost).

    This table below shows how you move up (←) or down (→) initially according to different finishes.

    Seed
    Finish
    1234
    1st← 2← 3← 4← 5
    2nd← 1← 2← 3
    3rd3 →2 →1 →
    4th5 →4 →3 →2 →

    The logic behind the movement is that the 1st seed (highest ranked player) is expected to win the group and therefore moves up less for doing so. If the 1st seed gets last in the group however, they are losing to 3 lower ranked players and therefore should be dropped below all of them. The same concept applies for the other 3 seeds. This system allows for more movement when the rankings are inaccurate and should result in less movement as the rankings start to become more accurate.

    2. Mid-way through the season, the formula will switch to that depicted below, which is more suitable once everyone has approached their appropriate position in the Ladder Standings and less movement is optimal.

    Seed
    Finish
    1234
    1st← 1← 2← 3← 4
    2nd1 →← 1← 2
    3rd2 →1 →← 1
    4th4 →3 →2 →1 →
    Seed
    Finish
    123
    1st← 1← 2← 3
    2nd1 →← 1
    3rd3 →2 →1 →

  2. The 'Players Playing Re-ranked' list (excluding any subs) forms the basis for the next round's Ladder Standings, but the players who did not play that round will be inserted into the new standings 1 place lower down then they were at the start of the round. For example, if the player ranked 13th in the Ladder Standings does not play one round, they will be the 14th ranked player the next round (unseating and bumping down whoever was previously occupying the 14th spot in the 'Players Playing Re-ranked' list).

  • Note: When you move up, you improve your position in the Standings (your ranking), but you will not necessarily find yourself in a higher group the next round, as groups are determined by a combination of rankings and availability.
