When:

  • This Winter Season starts on January 19th 2025, and runs through April 12th 2025.

Signing up:

  • Sign up here

Frequency:

In February:

  • Matches take place once every 2 weeks. A round is defined as one iteration of matches (2 weeks in this case).

  • Saturdays at 12:00 AM will mark the end of each round and the beginning of the next.

March - April:

  • Matches take place once every 1.5 weeks.

  • Saturdays and Wednesdays at 12:00 AM will alternately mark the end of each round and the beginning of the next.

Confirming Availability:

  • In order to be included in the roster of players playing each round, you will need to confirm that you are available before the round starts. You should consider yourself available if you will be able to offer a number of possible time-windows in which to play, and you will not be absent (traveling or otherwise unavailable) for any significant chunk of the round.

  • If you do not do so (or do not do so on time) for any given round, I will not be able to include you in that round's play.

Groupings:

  • Players will be grouped in groups of 4 at the start of each round. These are determined by rankings and availability. (Note: I use the terms 'rankings' and 'Standings' interchangeably here. Ranking essentially means position in the Standings).

    Group 1 will be comprised of the top 4 (4 highest-ranked) available players. Group 2 will be the next 4, and so on. (Note: if the number of available players is not a multiple of 4, the last group will have to be completed with subs).

  • Groupings can be found on the Groups Page. Look for them to be updated on the first day of each round around noon or early afternoon – you will be notified (by email) as soon as they are ready.

Scheduling:

  • Once you receive the email each round that tells you the Groups have been formed, you will open the Groups Page (the link to which will also be included in the email). Identify your group and fellow competitors for the current/approaching round. After having done so, please initiate communication with everyone in your group via a single all-person email or text thread to offer/suggest playing times and locations that will work for all 4 of you, taking into account anticipated weather. Scheduling your match for sooner rather than later is better in case weather causes postponement.

  • If you are unable to land on a viable time for all 4 of you, your group can get a sub (or multiple subs if necessary) to replace whoever is least available (or whoever's availability is the least in sync with everyone else's). A sub may also be called in if a player has not responded to the others' efforts to schedule. However, please give all players in your group reasonable opportunity – at least a couple of days – to communicate and offer times before proceeding to the sub step.

  • As the Ladder Administrator, I expect all players to have respectful communication and to be able to resolve scheduling issues/disputes amongst themselves. I do not want to have to get involved! If it does reach that stage, please start by sharing the all-person email thread or a picture of the text thread that you have used to SPLSchedulingDisputes_Emergency_Only@riseup.net.

  • Special note about Scheduling ➕
  • Special note about Scheduling ➖

    • Ladder play does not have to happen at off-peak hours. You are perfectly entitled to play your Ladder matches between 9am and 12pm at the public courts — i.e. during what is considered "peak time" — when there are cooler temperatures and typically less wind. So by all means offer morning times in the scheduling process.

    At any time, if all courts are full however, please note that you must be willing to rotate off between games. And then you can rotate back on (probably within just a few minutes) to the next available court.

    Further, the City of Santa Rosa policy is that "The courts are open for all to play on during peak hours... the courts must be shared, but it does not dictate who or how people play. [Players] must rotate off the court after one game but can rotate back in as a group ... [The policy] acknowledges that not everyone wants to rotate in with people they do not know or people at another level of play."

    Court capacity at Finley, especially during weekday mornings, is now substantially increased with the addition of 6 more cross-lined courts with high-quality nets available for use.

Unforeseen Circumstances:

    If one or more players in your group get sick, injured, or are otherwise unable to play due to exceptional circumstances, the following applies:

  • The missing players drop 1 place in the Ladder Standings for the following round (as they would if they had otherwise not been available).

  • The rest of you should try to a get a sub (and reschedule if necessary). If this is not possible — perhaps it is the last day of a round (hint: please don't schedule your match for the last day of a round) – your group will not play and everyone in it will drop 1 spot in the Standings).

Sub(stitute)s:

    As I'm sure you gathered from reading the above, there will inevitably be groups that end up needing subs throughout the course of the Ladder Season.

  • Subs do not have to be in the Ladder, but should ideally be of a similar skill level.

  • I have made a Subs Page to help facilitate the process of finding a sub. Anyone interested in subbing for a particular round can submit their name, and you can see everyone who is offering.

  • When submitting scores, please name the sub as 'FirstName LastName (Sub)' (replacing the name of the person they subbed in for in the appropriate cell of the spreadsheet if submitting via spreadsheet). Example: John Doe (Sub).

  • Subs' scores do not count for those they are subbing in for. (Remaining players' scores count as normal).

  • In exceptional circumstances, I as the Ladder Administrator may allow a sub's scores to count for that sub if they were unable to play in their own group, but were able to fill in for a missing player in an adjacent group.

Punctuality:

  • Please don’t be late! Be conscious that others in your group have arranged their schedule to play at that specific time. If you’re late, you may be significantly inconveniencing or frustrating them.

  • To be fair to all the other players, unless it’s an emergency, or there are extenuating circumstances, the following penalties apply for arriving late past the scheduled start time:

    15-30 mins late :: your opponents get a 3 point head start in every game to 11 (a 5 point head start in every game to 15).

    30-45 mins late :: your opponents get an 5 point head start in every game to 11 (a 8 point head start in every game to 15).

    45+ mins late :: your opponents get an 7 point head start in every game to 11 (a 11 point head start in every game to 15).

    No-show :: you will not be able to play the next round. (Please report to me if anyone in your group is a no-show so I can enforce this).

Format:

  • Round-robin, 6 games to 11, win by 2, (don't switch sides mid-game – see below).

  • If all 4 players in your group agree to it, you may play 3 games to 15 (switch sides at 8), or 3 games to 11 (switch at 6).

  • The #1 seed highest ranked player (order of player rankings on the Groups Page is highest to lowest from left to right) shall play with the #4 seed first game, then with the #3 seed, and lastly with the #2 seed.

  • In the default 6 games to 11 format, play in the this order, before repeating the sequence, and play on the opposite side of the court the second time you play with the same person.

Submitting Your Scores:

  • Please keep track of your scores after each game finishes and make sure everyone in your group agrees with them at the end of play.

  • It is every group's responsibility to submit their scores before the end of each round, as they are used to form the rankings for the next round. (You may want to nominate one person from your group to do the submission).

  • Failure to do so will result in all players in the group dropping one ranking spot (the same as if they hadn't played).

  • Since nobody wants this to happen, it is a good practice to submit them the same day right after playing.

  • To submit scores, please visit the Submit Scores Page. You will have the choice to submit through a form submission or to enter them into the Scores Spreadsheet. Submitting by spreadsheet is preferable if you have access to a computer (it is not very usable on a phone). However, beware that it may take a minute to load.

  • Please look at the Scores Spreadsheet the day before the start of the next round if possible to check that your group's scores appear correct. Once the next round starts, they may not be able to be changed.

Moving Up or Down:

  • New rankings will be formed each round based on the previous round's results.

  • There are two operations which are performed to calculate the new rankings:

  1. Firstly, the players who played that round are re-ranked based on their seed number (relative rank within group) and how they finished in their group (see 'Players Playing Re-ranked' rows in the Results sheet). You finish 1st(/4th) in your group by having the highest(/lowest) point differential (points won – points lost).

    This table below shows how you move up (←) or down (→) according to different finishes.

    1st← 2← 3← 4← 5
    2nd← 1← 2← 3
    3rd3 →2 →1 →
    4th5 →4 →3 →2 →

    The logic behind the movement is that the 1st seed (highest ranked player) is expected to win the group and therefore moves up less for doing so. If the 1st seed gets last in the group however, they are losing to 3 lower ranked players and therefore should be dropped below all of them. The same concept applies for the other 3 seeds. This system allows for more movement when the rankings are inaccurate and should result in less movement as the rankings start to become more accurate.

    2. Note: Part way into the season, I may switch to the formula shown in this table below, which is pertinent for once everyone has approached their appropriate position in the Ladder Standings and less movement is optimal.

  2. The 'Players Playing Re-ranked' list (excluding any subs) forms the basis for the next round's Ladder Standings, but the players who did not play that round will be inserted into the new Standings so that they end up 1 place lower down then they were at the start of the round. For example, if the player ranked 13th in the Ladder Standings does not play one round, they will be the 14th ranked player the next round (unseating and bumping down whoever was previously occupying the 14th spot in the 'Players Playing Re-ranked' list).

  • Note: When you move up, you improve your position in the Standings (your ranking), but you will not necessarily find yourself in a higher group the next round, as groups are determined by a combination of rankings and availability.
